Key Takeaways

01From Chips to AI Factories
Old computing = warehouses (store and retrieve). New computing = factories (produce tokens). NVIDIA designs entire racks as single systems: GPU, CPU, NVLink, memory, cooling, power. "Extreme Co-Design." Problems exceed single GPUs, Moore's Law is slowing.
024 Scaling Laws
Pre-training (reading): more data = smarter. Post-training (augmentation): synthetic data. Test-time (thinking): reasoning during inference, 100x more compute. Agentic (multiplication): sub-agents for complex workflows. Punchline: compute is the bottleneck, not data.
03The CUDA Moat
NVIDIA's moat isn't chips. It's the CUDA install base. Millions of devs trust code written for CUDA will work for decades. To build this, Jensen put CUDA on every GeForce, crushing margins to $1.5B market cap. Deep learning was discovered on those gaming cards. $1.5B to $4T.
0460 Reports, Zero 1-on-1s
60+ direct reports, no 1-on-1s. Group reasoning sessions. "Shaping belief systems." "Speed of light thinking" = measure against physical limits, design backward from physics. "Systematic forgetting" = deliberately forget past solutions to avoid anchoring.
05Supply Chain on Trust
Vera Rubin rack: 1.3M components, 2-3 tons, shipped fully assembled. 200+ suppliers. TSMC partnership on "trust without a contract." Assembly moved from data centers to factories with gigawatt-scale power testing.
06Intelligence as Commodity
AI factories produce tokens like factories produce goods. Agentic AI is the "iPhone moment" for token consumption. Global GDP on compute grows 100x. Intelligence becomes commodity. What doesn't? Humanity: character, compassion, generosity.
